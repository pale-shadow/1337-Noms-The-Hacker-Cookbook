# @kamikazi's Val's Chicken Chili

- Contest: Hack My Chili 2026
- Credit: @kamikazi
- Style: Mole
- Heat: mild
- Yield: 12-14 (see notes)
- Prep time: 45min
- Cook time: 1 hour minimum

## Ingredients

- 5T Olive oil, divided
- 4c chopped onion
- 8 cloves chopped garlic
- 4c sweet red peppers, diced
- 4 Jalapeños, cleaned and diced
- 4lbs chicken thighs, cut into 3/4" cubes
- 5T Chili powder
- 2.25t cumin seeds
- 1.5t ground coriander
- .25t ground cinnamon
- 6.5oz black olives, sliced
- 1.5c dark beer (stout or amber)
- 3/8c grated unsweetened chocolate (about 1 oz)
- .5t salt
- 2c corn kernels
- 28oz canned crushed tomatoes in puree
- 29oz canned diced tomatoes (drained)
- optional:
- 2c diced carrots
- garnishes:
- cilantro
- sour cream
- grated cheddar cheese
- sliced scallions
- diced avocado
- diced tomato
- black olives
- corn

## Instructions

1. Heat 3T olive oil in a dutch oven under high heat.  Add onions and garlic, sautee 5 minutes until soft and fragrant
2. Add red and jalepeno petters and sautee over medium heat about 10 minutes  (add diced carrots if using)
3. Stir in Chili powder, cumin, coriander, and cinnamon, sautee another 5 min.  Remove from heat
4. Lightly salt the chicken, and place a large skillet over medium-high heat
5. Add remaining olive oil and brown chicken until just cooked through.  You may need to batch the chicken to avoid crowding the pan
6. Add chicken, tomatoes, olives, and beer to the dutch oven / veggie mixture, and return to medium heat
7. Simmer over medium heat for at least 15 minutes, longer if you have the time
8. Before serving, add the grated chocolate, then season to taste with salt
9. While simmering, dice and prepare your garnishes.
10. This recipe easily scales up or down.

## How I Hack My Chili

The use of chocolate adds an unexpected richness to the tomato base without adding sweetness.  You can use a 70%-90% chocolate without too much sweetening effect, but unsweetened is best.

Serving is best done in a crock pot or chafing dish to ensure temperature.  If this is not available, you can use the alternate method:
Hardware:
1x 5 gallon bucket
16lbs anhydrous Calcium Chloride pellets (recommend Safe Step 7300.  Some ice melts are MgCl2 blends, which do not produce as much heat.  Ensure your selection is 100% CaCl2)
2 wooden paint stirrers
rubber dishwashing gloves and eye protection
duct tape
insulating medium (a hotel towel is fine)
aluminum foil
gallon-size freezer ziploc bags
optional: thermometer

This heating method is recommended for 1/2 recipe.  If you are making a full recipe, you may need to double

1. Place the chili into a gallon size ziplock bag.  Squeeze out all of the air, and then double-bag it. Set aside
2. Add 3 gallons of water to the 5-gallon plastic bucket and wrap in insulation.  If you have a thermometer to monitor the water temperature, add that to the system as well
3. Wearing gloves and eye protection, slowly add about 6 pounds of ice melter to the water, stirring constantly.  This should be enough to reach 140F.  Add the bag of chili
4. The water temperature will begin to drop, depending on the temperature of your chili.  Add another 2lbs of ice melter to bump the temperature back up, stirring constantly
5. Cover with aluminum foil or another insulating layer to retain heat
6. Monitor the water temperature, aiming to keep between 140F and 160F.  Add additional ice melt when needed

SAFETY NOTES!
- CaCl2 is very hydroscopic, and will dry out your hands if you handle it.  Remember to wear gloves
- NEVER ADD WATER TO CaCl2 - only add CaCl2 to water.  Adding water to CaCl2 can cause localized heating / boiling
- STIR and add slowly to the water to avoid clumping, which can result in uneven heating or waste of ice melt
- When disposing the used brine, flush the drain with additional water to ensure that any metal drain piping is not damaged by the salt.  If you are disposing outdoors, do not empty on any greenery or an area where you would like grass or plants to grow
