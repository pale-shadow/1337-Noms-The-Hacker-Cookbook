# @madstringer's 7-Pepper Pork Green Chili

- Contest: Hack My Chili 2026
- Credit: @madstringer
- Style: Green Chili
- Heat: Depends on Chili choices - 2-4 out of 5
- Yield: 8-12
- Prep time: 2 Hours
- Cook time: 1 Hour

## Ingredients

- 1-2 Tbls canola oil
- 2 lbs cubed pork shoulder
- salt and pepper to taste
- 1 large yellow onion
- 8 cloves of garlic, minced
- 2 C of chopped roasted assorted peppers consisting of:
  - poblanos (mandatory)
  - jalapeños
  - habaneros
  - serranos
  - chipotle
  - anaheim
  - ancho
  - cayenne
  - (or any combination of poblanos and 6 other peppers from this list: <http://chilipeppermadness.com/chili-pepper-types.html>)
- 1 - 14.5 oz can of diced tomatoes with juice (seasoned/spicy diced tomatoes work well)
- 1.5 C tomatillo salsa
- 5 C chicken broth (Better than Bouillon works well)
- 1/2 tsp dried oregano
- 1 pinch ground cloves

## Instructions

1. ROASTING THE CHILIS:
2. Pre-heat oven to Broil (or Low Broil if that is an option)
3. Place peppers on lightly greased baking sheet and broil for 10-15 minutes per side, until skins are reasonably charred. Upon removing from oven, place peppers in a 1 gallon freezer ziplock bag (double-bagging is recommended). Let sit for 15 minutes allowing peppers to steam inside the bag. Remove peppers from bag, and (gloves recommended) remove skin/stems/seeds (remove seeds consistent with the level of heat that you want).
4. MAKING THE GREEN CHILI:
5. Heat canola oil in a large (10 qt) stock pot over med-high heat. Season the pork shoulder cubes with salt and pepper generously. Place the cubes in hot oil and cook until golden brown on all sides, approximately 7 min. Once browned, remove pork from pot and set aside. Reduce heat to medium and stir in onion and garlic. Cook and stir until the onion has softened and turned translucent, about 3-5 minutes.
6. Return the pork to the pot and stir in the prepared chilis, can of diced tomatoes with juice, tomatillo salsa, and chicken broth. Season with oregano and cloves. Bring to a simmer over med-high heat, and then reduce heat to med-low. Cover and simmer for 20 minutes.
7. After 20 minutes, remove 2 cups of the "soup" (make sure there are no pork cubes in it) and pour into blender. Hold down the lid of the blender with a hot-pad/kitchen towel and puree the mixture until smooth. Pour the puree back into the pot - this will create a thicker texture and eliminate some of the chunky bits of chili. Continue to simmer, stirring occasionally until the pork is very tender, at least 35 minutes more.
8. Enjoy!
9. WASH EVERYTHING THE CHILIS TOUCH!

## How I Hack My Chili

Use any peppers you want! Roast your peppers in the oven! Definitely use a blender - this makes the chili thicker and hotter!

## Serving Notes

Eat with anything!

## Dietary Notes

Pork/Chicken Broth - Meat
Garlic/Onion - for those with an Allium allergy
Peppers - HOT!!
